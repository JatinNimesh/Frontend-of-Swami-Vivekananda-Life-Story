Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda (born Narendranath Datta on January 12, 1863, in Kolkata, India – died July 4, 1902) was a spiritual leader, philosopher, and a key figure in the introduction of Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. He was a chief disciple of Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, and he founded the Ramakrishna Mission in 1897 to promote spiritual development, education, and social welfare.

About This Legend

  • 👪 Real Name: Narendranath Datta.
  • 🧠 Known for: Spiritual leader, philosopher, monk, and reformer.
  • 🙏 Guru: Disciple of Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa.
  • 🌍 Famous Speech: At the World’s Parliament of Religions, Chicago, 1893.
  • 🕉️ Taught: Vedanta, Yoga, religious harmony, and self-realization.
  • 🇮🇳 Founded: Ramakrishna Mission (1897) for social service and spiritual growth.
  • ✊ Inspired Youth: Encouraged self-confidence, strength, and service to humanity.
  • 📅 National Youth Day: Celebrated on his birthday – January 12.
  • ⚰️ Died: July 4, 1902, at the age of 39.
